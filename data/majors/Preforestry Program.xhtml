Preforestry Program

CASNR offers a two-year preforestry program but does not offer a four-year degree. Students must transfer at the end of the freshmen or sophomore year to a forestry program accredited by the Society of American Foresters in order to obtain a professional forestry degree. An agreement with the University of Missouri-Columbia allows Nebraska residents with the proper scholastic qualifications to enter that institution without paying out-of-state fees. Students interested in transferring to a school other than the University of Missouri should obtain information about the school’s entrance requirements as early as possible to avoid unnecessary loss of credit.

The preforestry curriculum consists of 60-70 hours selected with the assistance of the student’s academic adviser and based on their background and career objectives.

Typical courses include:

BIOS 101 & BIOS 101L General Biology & Lab

BIOS 109 General Botany

BIOS 112 & BIOS 112L Intro to Zoology & Lab

CHEM 109 & CHEM 110 General Chemistry I & II

COMM 109 Fundamentals of Human Communication or COMM 209 Public Speaking

ENGL 150 Writing: Rhetoric as Inquiry or ENGL 254 Writing & Communities

MATH 104 Calculus for Managerial & Social Science or MATH 106 Analytic Geometry & Calculus I

NRES 103/AGRI 103 Intro to Agricultural & Natural Resource Systems

PHYS 141 Elementary General Physics I or PHYS 151 Elements of Physics

SOIL 153 Soil Resources

All of these courses apply to a degree in natural resources for those students choosing to stay at the University of Nebraska and change majors to another degree program in the School of Natural Resources.
