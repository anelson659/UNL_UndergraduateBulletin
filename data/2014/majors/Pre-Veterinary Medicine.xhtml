Individuals wishing to enter the four-year professional curriculum leading to the degree Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM) degree, must first complete two or more years of pre-veterinary education. Courses taken during pre-veterinary education must satisfy the entrance prerequisites for the college of veterinary medicine of the person’s choice. At the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, it usually requires a minimum of three years to complete the pre-professional requirements.

The Pre-Veterinary Program IS NOT a degree-granting program. Completion of the pre-veterinary program alone in fulfilling the prerequisites for admission to a college or school of veterinary medicine does not result in the awarding of a degree from the University of Nebraska. Students are encouraged to consider courses of study with a degree-granting program in veterinary science, veterinary technology, animal science, food science and technology, biochemistry or other fields compatible with the pre-veterinary program. Students can also complete pre-veterinary prerequisites with a degree program in wildlife or environmental studies, leading to a bachelor of science degree. It is also possible to pursue these pre-veterinary requirements in degree-granting programs outside the College of Agricultural Sciences and Natural Resources, such as the College of Arts and Sciences.

The program utilizes an “Advising Plus” model which involves faculty from the School of Veterinary Medicine and Biomedical Sciences and the Department of Animal Science.  Pre-veterinary  advising will guide students toward successful completion of requirements and application to veterinary school.  Advisors will also expose students to opportunities in animal health and assist them in identifying an appropriate degree program as they progress in their studies.

Students interested in veterinary medicine are encouraged to participate in the Pre-veterinary Club to learn about the exciting advancement and developments within the profession.

It is not recommended that pre-veterinary students take any courses on a Pass/No Pass basis (unless this is the only grading option for a given course, e.g. VBMS 101) because they may not be accepted for pre-professional prerequisites. Letter grades are required to evaluate credentials of applicants for veterinary college admission.
